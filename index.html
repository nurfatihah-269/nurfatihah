Mathematical Formulation & Derivative Derivation

The MD2 Pineapple Logistic Growth Curve

In bioscience, unlimited exponential growth is unrealistic due to nutrient, space, and biological limits. Therefore, a three-parameter Sigmoidal Logistic growth model is utilized to represent the cumulative fresh fruit weight of the MD2 pineapple.

Let \(f(t)\) be the cumulative fruit weight in grams at week \(t\) post-flower induction: $$f(t) = \frac{W_{\max}}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}} = \frac{1800}{1 + e^{-0.4(t - 10)}}$$

Parameter Key for MD2 Pineapple
  • \(W_{\max} = 1800\,\text{g}\): Carrying capacity representing the target maximum mature weight of a premium export Grade A pineapple fruit.
  • \(k = 0.4\,\text{wk}^{-1}\): Specific Growth Rate Coefficient representing growth velocity.
  • \(t_0 = 10\,\text{weeks}\): Inflection point representing the exact time of maximum instantaneous development.

Step-by-Step Analytical Derivative Derivation

To compute the rate of change of weight accumulation, we apply the chain and quotient rules to derive \(f'(t)\):

# Let u(t) = 1 + e^{-0.4(t - 10)}
f(t) = 1800 * [u(t)]⁻¹
# Apply the Chain Rule: f'(t) = -1800 * [u(t)]⁻² * u'(t)
u'(t) = d/dt [1 + e^{-0.4(t - 10)}] = -0.4 * e^{-0.4(t - 10)}
# Substitute back and simplify:
f'(t) = \frac{1800 * 0.4 * e^{-0.4(t - 10)}}{(1 + e^{-0.4(t - 10)})^2} = \frac{720 * e^{-0.4(t-10)}}{(1 + e^{-0.4(t-10)})^2}
Why This Matters to Agrotechnologists: The value \(f'(t)\) is the Absolute Growth Rate (AGR). It measures the grams of biomass added per week. When \(f''(t) > 0\), development is accelerating; when \(f''(t) < 0\), development is decelerating towards final ripening.
